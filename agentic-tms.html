Core Capabilities

Everything a TMS does —
except a human runs it.

Hubs, fleet, drivers, ingestion, routing, exception handling. All the building blocks of modern logistics, redesigned around autonomous agents from day one.

Hub Management

Real-time inventory, capacity, and throughput across every fulfilment node. Agents reroute parcels between hubs to clear bottlenecks before they form.

  • Live capacity heatmap across all hubs
  • SKU-level inventory & movement
  • Auto-rebalancing across nearest hubs
  • SLA breach prediction & pre-emption

Fleet Command

Vehicles, trucks, bikes, EVs — tracked, scored, and dispatched. Agents predict downtime, schedule maintenance, and pull underperformers off the road.

  • Live vehicle telemetry & geofencing
  • Utilisation, idle & fuel-burn scoring
  • Predictive maintenance windows
  • Mixed-mode fleet (own + 3PL + EV)

Driver Operations

Onboarding, attendance, shift planning, performance — all handled by agents. Drivers get the next-best trip; you get a self-managing field force.

  • Shift & roster auto-planning
  • Performance scorecards per driver
  • Incentive & payout reconciliation
  • In-app comms & escalation

Ingestion Pipeline

Plug in any order source — Shopify, marketplaces, EDI, custom APIs, CSV drops. Veltrum normalises, deduplicates, and enriches every record before it hits the network.

  • 40+ source connectors & SDK
  • Schema-flexible ingestion (JSON, EDI, XML)
  • Address validation & geocoding
  • Idempotent retries & dead-letter queues

Agentic Routing

Routes aren't computed once a day — they're continuously re-evaluated by agents reacting to weather, traffic, capacity, and SLA risk in real time.

  • Multi-leg, multi-modal optimisation
  • Live re-routing on disruption events
  • Cost vs. SLA vs. CO₂ trade-off control
  • Carrier allocation as part of routing

Issue Detection & Mitigation

Mistake Intel watches every shipment, every hop. It flags anomalies, root-causes them across systems, and either fixes them — or hands you a 1-click resolution.

  • Anomaly detection on every event
  • Cross-system root-cause traces
  • Auto-mitigation playbooks
  • Escalation with full audit trail

Autonomy you can trust

Eight guardrails,
built into the core — not bolted on.

Every agent operates inside the same governance frame. No exceptions, no off-switches buried in a config file. Your team always owns the steering wheel.

01
Scoped role definition
Every agent has a tightly defined job. Hub-Balancer cannot reroute a vehicle. Driver-Dispatch cannot approve an invoice. Scope is enforced at the orchestration layer, not the prompt.
02
Policy-grounded execution
Agents run on your SOPs, rate cards and SLA tables — versioned, reviewed and approved by your operations team. Updates are diffed and signed off before they go live.
03
Pre-launch simulation
1,000+ synthetic scenarios per agent before go-live — stress, edge, adversarial, regional. Scored by a panel of LLM judges plus your domain experts in the loop.
04
RBAC + tenant isolation
Permissions mirror your human org chart. Every action carries an actor, a tenant and a region. Cross-tenant data flow is impossible by design — enforced at the database row.
05
Three-tier confidence
Low-risk, high-confidence actions auto-execute. Medium presents a recommendation with evidence. Anything below the configured threshold escalates to a human — instantly.
06
Human approval for high-value moves
Carrier payouts, refund clusters, SLA waivers, contract amendments — every financial or contractual action above a configurable amount requires a named human approver. Always.
07
Real-time supervisor feedback
Managers can correct any decision in-line — accept, override, retrain. Feedback updates the policy layer immediately. The agent learns by Tuesday what you taught it on Monday.
08
Continuous performance reporting
Per-agent dashboards: completion rate, escalation frequency, confidence distribution, override count, dollar impact. Weekly digest in Slack, exportable to your BI stack.
